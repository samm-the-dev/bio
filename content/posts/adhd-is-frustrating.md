I woke up very early this morning with some ideas for my personal site that I found difficult to ignore. I thought maybe I'd gotten enough sleep to get through the day, so I got up and started working on them. I'm very proud of what I got done, and I got it done before my work day was supposed to start, but now it's past 5 PM and I didn't get much of my actual day job work done today.

I attended my morning meeting, and a check-in later in the afternoon, but I spent much of the day otherwise bouncing between social media platforms, watching a show, and taking a nap to catch up on sleep.

I wish I had the kind of job where I could just turn my phone off and more easily focus solely on my job, but 2FA precludes that at the moment. Focus Mode helps, but it's also very easy to dismiss/take "breaks". Should I get a second phone just for work? Bleh.

Working from home today didn't help either, especially since my dedicated office and work setup at home are somewhat dysfunctional at the moment. Fixing those is on my mental to-do list, but that's yet another unappealing task I have trouble engaging with. Things like this are the reason I started working on Ohm, my personal ADHD kanban app.

Ohm isn't done yet; I have a large refactor still pending, but I hope to get to that later in the week. I'm still proud of what I got done early this morning, so the day isn't a total wash, but it's frustrating how often I have days like this eating away at my PTO. My company has "floating holiday" PTO days I can use, so in honor of [National Get Over It Day](https://nationaltoday.com/national-get-over-it-day/) I think I'll try using one of those and just accept myself as a work in progress.

And I've made a LOT of progress in recent months.
